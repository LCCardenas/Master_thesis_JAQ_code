  • Can I take courses
    1. UCLouvain's web page

      *At LSM,* Exchange student may take up to 2 French courses for free (a basic ccourse and its complementary) if these ones are listed on their Learning Agreement. Be aware that language courses are not to be considered in the ECTS workload requirements of LSM.

    2. UCLouvain's web page

      Before the start of the semester
      If you are thinking of learning French, you can mention "French Course" without the course code in your Learning Agreement. You have the possibility to do a language test to evaluate your level of French when arriving in Belgium and thus select the course(s) you need.

    3. UCLouvain's web page

      *At LSM* If you are thinking of learning French, you can mention "French Course" without the course code in your Learning Agreement. You have the possibility to do a language test to evaluate your level of French when arriving in Belgium and thus select the course(s) you need.

    4. UCLouvain's web page

      Of course! (If you’re an ‘Erasmus+’ exchange student, you can even get a head start on improving your French before your programme begins, by taking online classes on the OLS platform – see next answer below). Intensive preparatory courses are offered to international students prior to the start of the academic year. After that, you can take classes at the UCLouvain Language Institute (Institut des langues vivantes, ILV) or improve your French via student organisations offering learning activities such as conversation groups, knowledge exchange, text correction, etc. Complete information on learning languages at UCLouvain.

    5. UCLouvain's web page

      Of course! If you want to improve your French during your master’s or doctoral programme, you can register for French classes at the UCLouvain Language Institute (Institut des langues vivantes, ILV). Moreover, the university, in collaboration with the Louvain Language Centres (Centres de Langues de Louvain, CLL), offers intensive French summer and winter holiday programmes specially designed for international students. More information on learning languages.

  • Available courses
    1. UCLouvain's web page (ILV French department)

      *At ILV* Courses are available from level A1 (beginners) to level C1 (advanced) in the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages. You will learn how to communicate in French orally and in writing by studying grammar and vocabulary.
      Our aim is to help you integrate into the life of the university and the town of Louvain-la-Neuve.
      All sorts of activities (exhibitions, movies, theatre, visits, talks, etc.) accompany the courses.
      Our team of teachers has developed its own unique pedagogical approach which is the result of its applied research in French as a foreign language.

  • Instruction language
    1. UCLouvain's web page

      The main language of instruction is French, and the university offers some programmes and classes in English. You can also take classes to get your French up to speed. With some exceptions, a doctorate can be pursued entirely in English.

    2. UCLouvain's web page

      Courses in other languages
      UCL offers master’s programmes that are taught partially or entirely in English. Some English-language courses are integral to degree programmes, mainly in the fields of law, economics, social sciences, political science, management, philosophy and letters, sciences, applied sciences, and health sciences.
      More details on course language and English-language course offerings are available in the course descriptions in the UCL course catalogue.
      Courses taught in Spanish, Italian and Dutch are limited to the field of philology.

    UCLouvain's web page

    For ‘Erasmus+’ students, yes: the Online Linguistic Support platform offers skills assessment in their programme’s main language of instruction.   • Enrol how

    UCLouvain's web page

    To enrol in a French course, you have to be :
    Regular UCLouvain Student or UCLouvain Doctoral student, Incoming Exchange Student at UCLouvain, Staff member at UCLouvain, or Researcher with a UCLouvain / FNRS / FRIA / FRESH scholarship.
    Come to an information session about the French courses, and/or go to the course(s) you are interested in as soon as possible. Take our placement test (organized during the 1st week of each semester). Clarify your status. Choose a course for your level. Go to class and check availability with the teacher. Enroll for class. According to your status, download the corresponding enrolment form. Fill in the personal information form and give it to your teacher.

  • Course organization
    1. UCLouvain's web page

      When do the classes and the exams take place?
      Classes start with the information session at the beginning of each semester. Class timetables: more details The exams take place about the middle of december (pre-session) and/or in January (January session); about the middle of May (pre-session) and/or in June (June session) and during the official period of the September session (second half of August) Exam timetables: more details

    2. UCLouvain's web page

      When do you need to register for the French exams in the ILV?
      Regular students (in a faculty) follow the enrolment procedures of their faculty.
      "Free" students and members of Personnel register by e-mail at the ILV secretariat during the registration periods: January session: mid-November; June session: mid-April; September session: beginning of July and the beginning of August.

  • Certificate

    UCLouvain's web page

    At the end of a course, how do you get a certificate or an attestation?
    A certificate is given to all “free” students who pass their exams with a minimum of 10/20. Send your request to emilie.bayens@uclouvain.be ; state the code number of the course, the semester and year, and your date and place of birth.
    An attestation is given to exchange students, in specific cases. You can request it as above.

  • Official tests

    UCLouvain's web page

    Where is there a DELF or a DALF examination centre?
    The Alliance française de Bruxelles and the University of Liège (Ulg) are exam centres for these tests.
    Our general courses prepare students to take these officiel tests.
